# TfL Refund Policy

## Incomplete journey refunds (most common)

When you tap in but fail to tap out, TfL charges you the maximum fare for the line you entered. You can claim a refund for the difference between the maximum fare and the fare you should have paid.

**How to claim:**
- Online: tfl.gov.uk/contact → "Refunds and Oyster" → "Oyster card journey refund"
- At any Tube station with an Oyster card reader (bring the card)
- You can claim up to **2 incomplete journey refunds per year** online. More than 2 require a phone call or visit to a station.
- Claims must be made **within 8 weeks** of the journey

## Overcharge refunds

If you were charged the wrong fare (e.g. railcard discount not applied), contact TfL:
- Online contact form: tfl.gov.uk/contact
- Customer services: 0343 222 1234

Provide: the date, time, and journey details. TfL can look up the exact charge on their system.

**For railcard issues:**
If your 26-30 railcard was registered to your Oyster card but discounts weren't applied, TfL will usually refund the difference going back several weeks, especially if the railcard was linked.

If the railcard was NOT registered to the Oyster card, you may not be able to claim retrospectively — but it's worth asking, especially if you have proof the railcard was valid.

## Daily cap refunds

TfL's systems automatically apply daily caps. If you believe a cap wasn't applied correctly, contact TfL with the specific dates.

## Delay repay / disruption refunds

If your journey was significantly delayed due to a TfL fault, you may be entitled to a refund:
- Tube delays: contact TfL customer services
- National Rail delays: use the train operator's delay repay scheme (separate from TfL)

## Duplicate charges

If your credit card shows multiple charges to TfL for the same day and amount when you only tapped up once, contact TfL immediately. This can happen with contactless (less common with physical Oyster).

## How to spot a maximum fare charge in your Oyster history

Maximum fare charges typically appear as:
- A journey entry with no end station (e.g. "Entered Brixton" with no corresponding exit)
- A very high charge that doesn't match the route you think you took
- A charge labeled "Incomplete journey"
- The Note field may say something like "No touch out"

## Time limits

- Incomplete journey refunds: 8 weeks
- Overcharge refunds: No strict limit, but harder to resolve after 3 months
- TfL keeps journey data for 8 weeks on Oyster (longer if registered online)
