# TfL Oyster/Contactless Fare Reference (2025/2026)

> Source: TfL official fare tables. Last updated March 2025. Verify at tfl.gov.uk/fares if auditing after a fare revision (fares typically change in March each year).

## Peak hours

- **Peak:** Monday–Friday, 06:30–09:29 and 16:00–18:59
- **Off-peak:** Before 06:30, 09:30–15:59, and after 19:00 on weekdays; all day on weekends and bank holidays

## 26-30 Railcard discount on TfL

- **1/3 off** off-peak Oyster fares on tube, Overground, DLR, and Elizabeth line
- Applies to **off-peak only**; during peak hours you pay the full adult rate (no discount)
- Does **not** apply to bus fares (bus is flat £1.75, frozen until July 2026)
- Does **not** apply to National Rail routes beyond the Oyster boundary
- Only works with a **physical Oyster card** — not contactless/Apple Pay/Google Pay
- Railcard must be **registered to the Oyster card** at a staffed Tube or Overground station
- If a journey was charged at the full adult rate when railcard should apply, the user was likely overcharged

**Quick calculation:** Multiply the adult off-peak fare by **0.666**, round **down** to nearest **5p**.

## Bus fares

- **Flat fare: £1.75** per journey (all zones, no railcard discount)
- Daily bus cap: **£5.25** (you won't pay more than this in a day for buses)
- Combined daily cap (bus + tube): see daily cap section

## Daily caps (2025)

TfL caps what you pay per day to prevent you being charged more than a daily Travelcard would cost.

| Zones | Peak cap | Off-peak cap |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| 1 only | £8.10 | £8.10 |
| 1–2 | £9.60 | £8.10 |
| 1–3 | £11.70 | £9.60 |
| 1–4 | £13.90 | £11.70 |
| 1–5 | £16.60 | £13.90 |
| 1–6 | £16.60 | £13.90 |

With 26-30 railcard, daily caps are also reduced by 1/3.

When a journey shows as **£0.00** with the note "This journey was cheaper or free today because you reached a daily cap" — this is correct behaviour, not an error.

## National Rail journeys via Oyster

Some National Rail routes within London accept Oyster but charge at National Rail fares, which may be higher than TfL tube fares:
- **St Pancras / King's Cross to Gatwick Airport**: NOT a standard Oyster journey. Gatwick is outside the Oyster zone. If this appears on an Oyster card, it was likely a through-ticketing arrangement or an error. National Rail fare from London to Gatwick is typically £10–£19 depending on the train.
- Journeys labeled "[National Rail]" in the journey description may legitimately cost more than tube fares.
